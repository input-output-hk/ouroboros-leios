-- Raw pushout
--
-- The category _‚äÜ_ does not have proper pushouts.  For instance consider:
--
--   œÑ·µ§ : [] ‚äÜ (u ‚à∑ [])
--   œÑ·µ• : [] ‚äÜ (v ‚à∑ [])
--
-- Then, there are two unrelated upper bounds (u ‚à∑ v ‚à∑ []) and (v ‚à∑ u ‚à∑ []),
-- since _‚äÜ_ does not include permutations.
--
-- Even though there are no unique least upper bounds, we can merge two
-- extensions of a list, producing a minimial superlist of both.
--
-- For the example, the left-biased merge would produce the pair:
--
--   œÑ·µ§‚Ä≤ : (u ‚à∑ []) ‚äÜ (u ‚à∑ v ‚à∑ [])
--   œÑ·µ•‚Ä≤ : (v ‚à∑ []) ‚äÜ (u ‚à∑ v ‚à∑ [])
--
-- We call such a pair a raw pushout.  It is then a weak pushout if the
-- resulting square commutes, i.e.:
--
--   ‚äÜ-trans œÑ·µ§ œÑ·µ§‚Ä≤ ~ ‚äÜ-trans œÑ·µ• œÑ·µ•‚Ä≤
--
-- This requires a notion of equality _~_ on sublist morphisms.
--
-- Further, commutation requires a similar commutation property
-- for the underlying equality _‚âà_, namely
--
--   trans x‚âày (sym x‚âày) == trans x‚âàz (sym x‚âàz)
--
-- for some notion of equality _==_ for equality proofs _‚âà_.
-- Such a property is given e.g. if _‚âà_ is proof irrelevant
-- or forms a groupoid.

    leg‚ÇÇ         : zs ‚äÜ upperBound

-- Upper bound of two sublists xs,ys ‚äÜ zs
--
-- Two sublists œÑ : xs ‚äÜ zs and œÉ : ys ‚äÜ zs
-- can be joined in a unique way if œÑ and œÉ are respected.
--
-- For instance, if œÑ : [x] ‚äÜ [x,y,x] and œÉ : [y] ‚äÜ [x,y,x]
-- then the union will be [x,y] or [y,x], depending on whether
-- œÑ picks the first x or the second one.
--
-- NB: If the content of œÑ and œÉ were ignored then the union would not
-- be unique.  Expressing uniqueness would require a notion of equality
-- of sublist proofs, which we do not (yet) have for the setoid case
